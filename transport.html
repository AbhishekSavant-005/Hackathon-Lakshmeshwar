TRANSPORT SCHEME

Still No More Schemes

A transport scheme is a plan or system for the movement of people or goods from one place to another.
It involves various modes of transportation, such as buses, trains, cars, planes, and ships, and may also include infrastructure such as roads, railways, airports, and seaports.
A transport scheme is designed to meet the specific transportation needs of a particular region or community, taking into account factors such as population density, traffic patterns, and economic activity. It may involve the development of new transportation infrastructure,
Transport schemes can have a significant impact on the economy, the environment, and quality of life. A well-designed transport scheme can improve access to jobs, education, and services, reduce traffic congestion and air pollution, and support economic growth. However, poorly designed transport schemes can have negative impacts,
such as increased traffic congestion, air pollution, and social exclusion.
